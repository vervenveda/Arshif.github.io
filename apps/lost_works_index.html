The Vault

ARSHIF · Palimpsest Vault of Lost & Hidden Works

A quiet register of works that have been burned, buried, fragmented, or pushed to the edges of public memory. Some survive only in scraps, some in translations, some in the whisper of a title. This Vault does not erase anyone’s canon — it simply remembers.

Lost from Antiquity

Aeschylus’ Lost Plays

Ancient Greek tragedy · 5th century BCE

Only a handful of Aeschylus’ tragedies survive. Dozens of others — titles, themes, and characters — are known only through quotations and later references.

Sources: Surviving plays (Aeschylus PDF)

Lost from Antiquity

Persica of Ctesias

History of Assyria & Persia · c. 5th–4th century BCE

A sprawling account of Assyrian and Persian courts, now lost in its original form. Only fragments and retellings hint at what once stood on its pages.

Sources: Ctesias’ Persica and its Near Eastern Context

Lost from Antiquity

The Hellespontine Sibyl

Oracular voice · Asia Minor

A prophetic figure whose sayings were once consulted like oracles. Her written collections have slipped beyond reach, leaving only echoes in scattered references.

Sources: ToposText entry on the Hellespontine Sibyl

Lost from Antiquity

Anaxagoras’ Book of Philosophy

Pre-Socratic philosophy · 5th century BCE

Known for introducing “Nous” (Mind) as a cosmic ordering principle. Only small fragments remain — the rest is a silhouette traced by later writers.

Sources: Anaxagoras – Philosophy (study PDF)

Lost from Antiquity

Lost Mexican Codices

Aztec & Maya books · pre-colonial Americas

Countless painted manuscripts were destroyed during conquest — recording astronomy, law, ritual, and history. Only a small remnant of codices survived the fires.

Sources: Aztec Codex (Archaeological Institute of America)

Fragmented & Partial

The Sibylline Books

Oracular manuscripts · Ancient Mediterranean

A legendary set of prophetic collections once guarded in Rome. Fires, disasters, and purges left only fragments and later Christian redactions behind.

Sources: The Sibylline Oracles (Sacred-Texts PDF)

Fragmented Scripture Tradition

The Book of Enoch

Second Temple Jewish literature

Preserved more fully in Ethiopic tradition, echoed in fragments and quotations elsewhere. For many, it lives in a borderland — partly present, partly lost, always debated.

Sources: The Book of Enoch (Global Grey eBook)

Lost & Apocryphal Texts

Lost Gospels & Proto-texts

Early Christian writings · various communities

Titles like the Gospel of Mary, the Protevangelion, and other early-Christian writings inhabit the margins — some partially preserved, some missing entirely, many labeled “apocryphal” by later canons.

Sources: The Apocryphal Gospels: Texts and Translations (Ehrman & Pleše)

Banned & Challenged

The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn

Mark Twain · 1884

Frequently challenged for its language and portrayals of race, this novel moves in and out of school lists and library shelves, reminding us that “forbidden” and “forgotten” are not the same.

Sources: Full text PDF (AmericanEnglish.state.gov)

Banned & Challenged

To Kill a Mockingbird

Harper Lee · 1960

A modern classic about justice, race, and conscience. Frequently challenged or removed from curricula, yet still widely read — a reminder that some “vaults” are legal and cultural, not physical fire.

Sources: Full text PDF (raio.org)
