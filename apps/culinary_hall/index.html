Food Safety Chamber

The non-negotiable foundation. Use this as a teaching room and quick reference, then verify with current official sources before cooking for vulnerable people, selling food, canning, or serving groups.

Core Pattern: Clean · Separate · Cook · Chill

  • Clean: wash hands, tools, boards, surfaces, and produce when appropriate.
  • Separate: keep raw meat, poultry, seafood, eggs, and their juices away from ready-to-eat foods.
  • Cook: use a food thermometer and current safe-temperature guidance.
  • Chill: refrigerate promptly; shallow containers help large batches cool faster.
  • Label: dish name, date, allergens, storage, reheat notes, and discard plan.

Major U.S. Food Allergens

Milk, egg, fish, Crustacean shellfish, tree nuts, wheat, peanuts, soybeans, and sesame. Always ask, label, prevent cross-contact, and take allergic reactions seriously.
