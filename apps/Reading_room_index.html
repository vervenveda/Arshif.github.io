Reading Room

ARSHIF · The Lost & Hidden Archives of Literature

A quiet catalog of remembered scrolls — some revered, some disputed, all treated with care. This shelf is for study, comparison, and prayerful reflection — not for erasing anyone’s canon, but for seeing what history tried to write over.

Second Temple · Ethiopic

Complete Book of Enoch
by Dr. Jay Winter

Dream-visions, watchers, judgment, and cosmic order.

Preserved mainly in Ethiopic tradition; echoes in later Jewish and Christian thought about angels, the flood, and the Day of the Lord.

Second Temple · Hebrew (fragments)

Book of Jubilees
Ethiopian Text translated
by R.H. Charles, D.Litt., D.D.

Retells Genesis and Exodus through “jubilee” cycles.

Reframes early biblical history with angelic narration, covenant themes, and a sacred calendar that shaped community rhythms.

Deuterocanonical · Narrative

Tobit

Exile, healing, and angelic companionship.

A family story of loyalty and trust in God, woven with prayer, almsgiving, and the mysterious guidance of Raphael.

Deuterocanonical · Wisdom

Wisdom of Ben Sira (Sirach)

Ancestral wisdom, everyday holiness, and practical ethics.

A long stream of proverbs and reflections that honor Torah, family, community, and the fear of the Lord as the beginning of wisdom.

Deuterocanonical · Hellenistic

Wisdom of Solomon

Justice, immortality, and divine wisdom.

Written in a world of empires and philosophy, this book defends righteousness, meditates on the fate of the just and unjust, and portrays Wisdom as a radiant companion of God.

1–2 Esdras

Temple rebuilding, visions, and questions about justice.

Preserves alternative tellings of return-from-exile stories and probing dialogues about suffering, empire, and God’s timing.

Early Christian · Sayings

Gospel of Thomas

A collection of sayings attributed to Jesus.

Not a narrative gospel, but a string of parables and sharp sayings preserved in Coptic and Greek fragments, much debated and much studied.

The Shepherd of Hermas

Visions, parables, and calls to repentance.

Core Canon · Qur’an

The Holy Qur’an

Central scripture of Islam — recitation, guidance, and mercy.

Preserved and recited across centuries, the Qur’an stands as a living canon: a touchstone for language, devotion, and comparison when exploring other scrolls and traditions in the Palimpsest Library.
