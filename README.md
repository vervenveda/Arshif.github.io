## Purpose

ARSHIF exists to preserve, organize, connect, and make accessible the record of human knowledge.

The archive is intended to support:

- independent research;
- public education and lifelong learning;
- cultural, historical, artistic, scientific, and spiritual inquiry;
- public-domain discovery;
- source literacy and verification;
- interdisciplinary study;
- comparative research;
- preservation of oral traditions and human memory;
- careful examination of contested ideas;
- responsible use of historical and health information;
- free-access educational pathways; and
- learning outside commercial platform lock-in.

ARSHIF is not intended to be an endless feed of disconnected content.

It is an archive of relationships:

- between evidence and interpretation;
- between people and places;
- between discovery and consequence;
- between texts and the societies that transmitted them;
- between tradition and change;
- between memory and power;
- between disciplines that are too often separated; and
- between knowledge and public responsibility.

## Guiding Principles

### 1. Evidence

ARSHIF should distinguish among:

- primary sources;
- secondary scholarship;
- reference works;
- commentary;
- interpretation;
- tradition;
- oral testimony;
- public records;
- emerging research;
- disputed claims; and
- unsupported speculation.

Visitors should be able to understand what kind of source they are reading and where it came from.

### 2. Context

Knowledge should not be presented as an isolated fragment. Whenever practical, ARSHIF should identify relevant:

- historical context;
- cultural context;
- geographic context;
- scientific context;
- political context;
- ethical context;
- linguistic context;
- religious context; and
- limits or uncertainties.

### 3. Access

ARSHIF should offer a clear first doorway into difficult subjects through:

- readable explanations;
- thoughtful organization;
- public-domain works;
- free-access resources;
- plain-language pathways;
- accessible interfaces; and
- progressive levels of study.

### 4. Stewardship

Human stories, sacred traditions, cultural knowledge, historical records, artistic works, and community memory should be treated as inheritances to preserve—not as material to extract without care, context, credit, or responsibility.

### 5. Intellectual Independence

ARSHIF is designed to encourage investigation rather than passive acceptance. Visitors should be invited to:

- compare sources;
- examine evidence;
- recognize uncertainty;
- identify bias;
- distinguish fact from interpretation;
- preserve legitimate disagreement; and
- form conclusions carefully.

### 6. Human Dignity

Research involving people, cultures, health, trauma, identity, belief, conflict, or lived experience should be presented without humiliation, exploitation, sensationalism, or dehumanization.

### 7. Sovereignty

The archive should remain portable, understandable, preservable, and independently operable without mandatory accounts, hidden tracking, or unnecessary platform dependence.

Internal Application Records

the main application card;
the searchable hamburger menu;
category filters;

Connected Repository Records

Each entry in networkSites supplies information to:

the connected-site cards;
the searchable menu;

External Research Records

Each entry in researchResources supplies information to:

research-resource cards;

This central-directory approach reduces the chance that a card, menu item, and modal button will point to different locations.
